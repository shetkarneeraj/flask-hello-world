How to get started?

Instructions

Point the device to some horizontal surface: Ensure your device is set up correctly and the camera is ready.

Plus button: Pops up the sheet including the list of all the elements, click on either of them to add an element and its valence electrons to AR View.

Select the element to bond with: Either select the element from the top bar or just point the center circle to the element in AR.

Get more info: Point at the element to get more info about an element and click on the info button on the right sidebar.

Set angle and bond type: Set angle and bond type and plane before selecting another element in AR.

Bin button: Clears off the components in AR.

Annotate in AR: Use the annotation tool to add notes and labels directly in the AR view for better understanding and reference.

You can also find these instructions in the Instructions tab within the app.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

What is chemAR?

chemAR is an augmented reality application designed to enhance the learning experience of chemistry through interactive visualizations and quizzes.

How do I use the app?

Simply point your device at a horizontal surface, select elements to add to the AR view, and interact with them using the provided tools and features.

Is chemAR free to use?

Yes, chemAR is completely free to use with no in-app purchases or hidden fees.

Does chemAR collect any personal data?

No, chemAR does not collect any personal or usage data. All content and molecular models are stored locally on your device.

What devices are supported?

chemAR is designed to work on iOS devices that support ARKit. Please ensure your device is compatible with ARKit for the best experience.

Privacy Policy

chemAR Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how chemAR ("the App", "we", "our") handles data and protects your privacy. By using chemAR, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

Data Collection & Usage

chemAR processes and stores data sent via the description fields anonymously. This data is collected to monitor the outputs generated by the LLM models and improve their performance. The prompts sent via the Lab menu, along with the answers generated by the AI model, are securely stored in our database.

LLM Model & AI Outputs

chemAR utilizes the Gemini 2.0 Flash Lite LLM model to generate AI-driven responses. Users are advised that AI-generated answers may not always be accurate, and discretion is advised. The stored data is used solely for research purposes and not for any commercial or marketing activities.

User Caution & Confidentiality

Users are strongly advised not to enter any confidential, sensitive, or personally identifiable information in any text fields within the app. The data stored is anonymized and only used for improving AI capabilities.

No Personal Data Collection

chemAR does not collect personal information or track user activities. All content, molecular models, and AR interactions are stored locally on your device.

Camera Permissions & AR Functionality

To provide an immersive augmented reality experience, chemAR requires access to your device’s camera. The camera is used only for aligning and rendering AR elements, and no images or videos are captured, stored, or transmitted externally.

Educational Purpose Only

chemAR is designed exclusively for educational purposes. The app’s interactive molecular visualizations and augmented reality experiences are intended to support chemistry learning and comprehension.

Open Source Data

The data for chemical elements and molecular structures is sourced from reputable open data repositories. While we strive for accuracy, users are encouraged to verify any critical information independently.

Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically in response to legal, technical, or business developments. Changes will be posted within the app, and users are encouraged to review the policy regularly.
